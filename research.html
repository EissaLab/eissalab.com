01
Human Behavior

Diverse strategies in Adaptive Decision-Making

How is information relevant to our environment learned, processed, and applied to our everyday choices?

To study this, we build decision-making games with “hidden” features that change how a participant should behave. We then use mathematical modeling to determine what strategies humans use to change their decision-making in different environments.

02
Neural Mechanisms

Flexibility supported across brain regions

How does our brain balance adapting to potential new environments while performing well in our current environment, given how complex the world is?

We combine human neural activity recordings with neural network modeling to understand how the brain implements flexible decision-making strategies.

Efficient coding Neural networks Mechanisms
03
Theme 03

Cognition in Psychiatric Disorders

Can we understand how and why some patients with psychiatric and neurological disorders struggle with flexibly updating their choices?

We develop translational tools that can measure the decision-making strategies patients use, how these strategies differ from the general population, and whether these behavioral metrics can predict treatment outcomes.
